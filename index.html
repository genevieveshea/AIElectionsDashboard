About this monitor

A working dataset compiled from public sources (Freedom House, Europe Elects, IFES Election Guide, International IDEA, OSCE/ODIHR, EU AI Act tracker, CETaS at the Alan Turing Institute, and national election authorities). v1.1, 16 May 2026. The underlying spreadsheet is the source of truth and is updated periodically.

Method & definitions

Scope. 46 member states of the Council of Europe (Russia expelled 2022). Elections from 16 May 2026 through 31 December 2027. Both national and identifiable subnational/regional contests.

Readiness questions. Each scored Yes = 2, Partial = 1, No = 0, Unknown = 0. Maximum 10.

  • Election authority AI guidance? Has the EMB issued written rules or formal guidance on AI-generated content and deepfakes?
  • Media literacy mechanisms? Government-supported, sustained programmes building citizens' ability to identify AI-generated content.
  • Independent fact-checking present? Active, independent organisations (ideally IFCN signatories) operating at scale.
  • Platform engagement institutionalized? Formal, ongoing channel between EMB/government and major platforms — not ad-hoc.
  • Transparency mechanisms? Enforceable rules on political-ad disclosure, AI-content labelling, and source disclosure (EU AI Act Art. 50, DSA, TTPA, or national equivalents).

Tiers. Strong (8–10), Moderate (5–7), Weak (2–4), Very weak (0–1).

Freedom House. Status and 0–100 score from Freedom in the World 2026, released March 2026, covering events of 2025.

Caveats. Many entries are best-effort summaries from public sources, not vetted legal opinion. Treat as a starting point. Subnational coverage is selective.
